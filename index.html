Top Questions

Algorithm-Backed Answers

Each answer is grounded in the open-source codebase — not speculation.

Why does my reach drop after posting 3–4 times quickly?

The algorithm applies an exponential author diversity penalty when multiple posts from the same author appear in the same scoring batch. Your first post scores at full strength; your second at ~55%; your third at ~33%; your fourth at ~21%. This is called author diversity decay and it compounds — posting 5 times in a burst is algorithmically equivalent to about 1.4 posts worth of reach.

Does video actually help your reach?

Only if it clears the minimum duration threshold. The algorithm has a dedicated vqv (video quality view) signal, but it is gated — if your video is too short, the weight for that signal becomes zero. A video below the threshold earns no vqv credit and still risks the not_dwelled negative signal if viewers scroll past it.

→ Deep dive: content-format-guide.md

What is the difference between For You and Following algorithmically?

The Following tab sets in_network_only = true, which disables Phoenix OON retrieval entirely. Only Thunder (in-network posts from accounts you follow) serves candidates, sorted purely by recency. No ML scoring selects what appears — it is chronological within a 2-day window.

→ Deep dive: 00-overview.md

Does the "Not Interested" button actually hurt you?

Yes. not_interested_score is a negative-weight signal in the scoring formula. More importantly, posts that accumulate enough negative signals (not interested, mute, report) cross an offset normalization cliff — they are placed in a score range below all positive-scoring posts and cannot compete regardless of likes.

→ Deep dive: what-kills-your-reach.md

How does the algorithm decide to show your posts to non-followers?

Via Phoenix retrieval — a two-tower neural network that encodes your post into an embedding vector (computed offline by Grox) and matches it against users' interest embeddings. Consistent posting in a niche clusters your embeddings, making your posts easier to retrieve for similar audiences. Once retrieved, your post still has to survive an OON score penalty before winning a feed slot.

→ Deep dive: discovery-and-reach.md

What is the single most important signal to optimize for?

Replies — because they are high-friction (require composition), a strong indicator of genuine interest, and carry a dedicated positive weight. But the most damaging signal to avoid is not_dwelled — the passive negative signal that fires on every impression where the viewer scrolls past without stopping. Avoiding that signal is more important than chasing any single positive one.

→ Deep dive: max-engagement-playbook.md

Does deleting and reposting reset your distribution?

No. The delete event is processed by the infrastructure (Thunder) via Kafka. The repost gets a new Snowflake ID and zero inherited engagement. Engagement signals from the original post (replies, likes, retweets) do not transfer. You start from scratch, with none of the previous signal.

→ Deep dive: posting-cadence.md

How long does a post stay in the algorithm?

In-network distribution has a hard 2-day (172,800 second) cutoff — Thunder trims posts older than this from the serving store. The Phoenix pipeline's AgeFilter also hard-drops candidates beyond a configured max age. There is no gradual fade — it is a binary cutoff. Posts do not resurface algorithmically after aging out.

→ Deep dive: posting-cadence.md

Do hashtags help?

Not directly through a hashtag-specific signal in scoring — the algorithm does not have a hashtag weight. Hashtags can help indirectly through topic classification (filtered_topic_ids assigned to your post) and by attracting the right audience (reducing not_dwelled signals). However, commonly-muted hashtag terms will cause your post to be filtered out for viewers who have muted those keywords.

→ Deep dive: 08-filtering.md

Why do some replies get more reach than standalone posts?

They should not, structurally — replies face additional in-network filtering (Thunder only includes replies if the replied-to post is a root post, or the replied-to user is in the viewer's following list). However, replies to high-engagement threads inherit visibility from the parent thread's audience, which is a different distribution path than the algorithmic feed.

→ Deep dive: 03-thunder.md

Does your follower count affect how the algorithm weights you?

Not directly in the scoring formula — the scoring formula is purely engagement probability weighted sum. However, new accounts get routed to a different Phoenix retrieval cluster (new-user inference cluster) and receive a hardcoded OON weight factor that cannot be tuned at runtime. Establishing engagement history is the fastest path out of the new-user treatment.

→ Deep dive: discovery-and-reach.md

What can silently remove your post before anyone sees it?

Multiple pre-scoring filters run before ranking: AuthorSocialgraphFilter (mutes, blocks — bidirectional), MutedKeywordFilter (token-based keyword matching), AgeFilter (Snowflake-derived age), PreviouslySeenPostsFilter (bloom filter + exact ID). After ranking, VFFilter (post-selection) removes posts flagged by Grox's offline safety classifier — a post can score highly and still be removed.
